# Volume 3 - Website Designs

## Screen Production Target

The full design asset kit should produce 420 screen deliverables.

This count is achieved by designing screen families across responsive breakpoints and product states:

- Public platform: 24 pages x 5 variants = 120 screens.
- Sponsor journey: 16 pages x 6 variants = 96 screens.
- Mission Control: 18 views x 5 variants = 90 screens.
- AI suite: 10 views x 5 variants = 50 screens.
- Social, press, and content tools: 16 views x 4 variants = 64 screens.

Variants include desktop, tablet, mobile, loading, empty, error, modal, and high-value interaction states depending on the screen.

## Homepage Blueprint

Purpose:

Create immediate sponsor confidence and fan curiosity.

Above the fold:

- Adam Ellahi name as the primary signal.
- High-impact racing image or cinematic generated hero background.
- Current season status.
- Next race or most recent result.
- Sponsor credibility cue.
- Primary CTA: Partner With Adam.
- Secondary CTA: Explore The Journey.

Below the fold:

- Performance snapshot.
- Journey timeline.
- Race weekend story.
- Sponsor value module.
- Media proof.
- Social momentum.
- Latest news.
- Newsletter signup.

Mobile rules:

- One clear primary CTA.
- Hero must leave a hint of the next section.
- Do not bury result, next race, or sponsor CTA.

Every public page needs:

- Unique title.

- Structured data where relevant.
- Internal links.
- Author or entity references.
- FAQ where useful.
- Downloadable assets for press and sponsors.

## Responsive Rules

Mobile:

- Design from 390 px first.
- No text overlap.
- No horizontal scroll.
- One-thumb primary actions.
- Cards become vertical rows.
- Tables become comparison cards or scroll areas with clear affordance.

Desktop:

- Use 12-column layouts.
- Expand data density.
- Keep editorial content readable.
- Use side-by-side proof modules only where they improve comprehension.

Ultra-wide:

- Mission Control may expand to 16 columns.
- Public pages should constrain reading content and use environment/background expanses carefully.

## Screen Acceptance Criteria

A screen is accepted only when:

- It has clear business purpose.
- It works on mobile.
- It uses tokens.
- It includes accessibility states.
- It has real component names.
- It does not rely on placeholder filler.
- It adds sponsor, fan, media, or team value.
